The Story

Two Names, One Mission

Founded in December 2017, toN0tErr operates under two complementary brands:

toN0tErr

Tools to Nought Error — the umbrella brand encompassing all products, tools, and services aimed at driving errors toward zero. The “0” in the name represents the goal: zero errors.

CleaErr

Clear Errors — the consulting practice brand. Through CleaErr Tech Solutions, we embed into client engineering teams as Technical Consultants, Design Consultants, and Hardware Leads.

Every product, service, and consulting engagement we undertake shares a single purpose: to systematically reduce and eliminate errors. Errors can be human, machine, or system-originated. Our tools can be products, devices, processes, or applications. What unites them is the relentless drive toward zero.

Our Approach

Identify. Engineer. Eliminate.

A three-step methodology applied to every engagement, every tool, every solution.

1. Identify

Understand where errors originate. Is it a human process gap? A machine calibration drift? A system design flaw? Root cause analysis before solution design.

2. Engineer

Build tools, devices, processes, and applications that address root causes. Whether it's a hardware test jig, an AI-powered QC scanner, or an energy compliance platform — engineer the solution.

3. Eliminate

Drive error rates toward zero through deployment, monitoring, and continuous refinement. The goal isn't just detection — it's elimination.

Heritage

SCAD 508 — Technology Development Board Award

Led R&D and indigenization of the SCAD 508 strain measurement system at Pyrodynamics — for which the company received the Technology Development Board Award from the President of India, for successful indigenization and eliminating India's dependence on imported precision test equipment. The SCAD 508 was also the only Indian-developed instrument showcased at an international exhibition in Germany.

This recognition validates the "nought error" mission at its deepest level: precision instrumentation that meets international standards.
